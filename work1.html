ROOT-ME

UX

Made with Théo Geiller , Anaïs Zafra and Lisa Martel , L'École de Design / 2019

Redesign of a website of e-learning of hacking.
How to valorise the penibility of the learning ?
During a week of workshop , we redesigned the concept of the website root-me.org to create an approach of the learning of hacking fully-gamified. The user won't only have to take up a challenge, but will challenge others players / apprentice - hacker of same level.
Based on game environments of MMO, the "player" will be encourage to improve his / her skills in hacking at every moment of his day : to win against others, he / she have to know perfectly the knowkings of his level !
Here we based the experience of learning on the parallel between progress in game, and improving of hacking skills ; but especially on the access of the documentation, very important in this environment : no given answers to newbies, because only the self investment in looking for answers, and the penibility of this tasks makes it as much gratifying.
So the user interface is centered on the progress of the player with acces to his statistics from a lots of way, and the rarefaction of the documentation to give it a precius status, in order that the reading of those documentations became the goal of the "student".

Context

Musicians - and moreover self-taught people - are always looking for new tablatures, seeking for the ONE that is the most respectful of the original sound, one wich match with their guitar level or one wich offer an unconventional version. But this search could be laborious when they search a particular one, from a song not famous, or the live version of an awesome solo. Trying to write down his own tablature is often long and could be demoralizing for novices, who can not find a proper help to their issues.

Personas

We divided in three types of users that Root-me could welcome on its platform : the "newbie" wich never coded before and have not any knowing about web security and web languages. One user warned concerning web languages but not really about hacking. Lastly, the warned user in hacking who want to train himself / herself or become more expert in some field.

Audit

Root-me is a portal of hacking exercices for self-learning, which requires motivation lever to pursue the education alone. But its interface is difficult at first, with a side bar and a top bar to navigate between menus, and a lot of submenus inside them. Whereas the documentation is central in this type of learning, it is not the first item that user can found on a exercice page. Gamified aspect is not fully exploited, with only few statistics at the end of the exercice. Finally, the community is only present of the chatbox on the side, whereas could be a motivation lever for hackers students.

Ideation

Because Root-me is a self-learning portal, we focused on the newer users that the platform could welcome.

Tabmaker will be a service wich provide a solution for many guitar players and could be a new platform of self-promotion for profesionnals musicians : a sharing platform wich integrates an collaborative tool to write new tablatures or edit old ones.
The editor will be a collaborative tool, where many users could work on it at the same time. Due to its collaborative aspect, the tablature editor should have a functionnality that permits users to write many versions of a same part of the partition ; in order to let the choice to readers between them.
The chat, always present in the navigation, allows editors to talks about their work while they are writing, readers to ask some tips / help, and even more.

Research & UX

I started by a market research wich revealed that some actors had developped desktop apps for tablature editing, and many web portals of pre existing partitions already exist. So, it is time to blend them and create a collaborative tool !
I started by listing every necessary functionnality for a collaborative tool for partitions, and then add some usefull features, as a weekly vote to choose the next tab which will be edited on the coming community rendez-vous. I fixed the main navigation on the website, and the hierarchization of functionnalities units to reduce the cognitive burden of this web app.

To proceed efficiently and quickly in the short time we had left, we work by periods of alone work, and group consultations to vote for the best idea concerning a functionality or the future axe we had to work on.

User tests

After finalized a prototype on Sketch, we tested it with some users in order to observe if they easily navigate in our service, if they understand well our entitles... We required that they verbalize their feelings during the test.
