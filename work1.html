ROOT-ME

UX

Made with Théo Geiller , Anaïs Zafra and Lisa Martel , L'École de Design / 2019

Redesign of a website of e-learning of hacking.
How to valorise the penibility of the learning ?
During a week of workshop , we redesigned the concept of the website root-me.org to create an approach of the learning of hacking fully-gamified. The user won't only have to take up a challenge, but will challenge others players / apprentice - hacker of same level.
Based on game environments of MMO, the "player" will be encourage to improve his / her skills in hacking at every moment of his day : to win against others, he / she have to know perfectly the knowkings of his level !
Here we based the experience of learning on the parallel between progress in game, and improving of hacking skills ; but especially on the access of the documentation, very important in this environment : no given answers to newbies, because only the self investment in looking for answers, and the penibility of this tasks makes it as much gratifying.
So the user interface is centered on the progress of the player with acces to his statistics from a lots of way, and the rarefaction of the documentation to give it a precius status, in order that the reading of those documentations became the goal of the "student".
