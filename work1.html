ROOT-ME

UX

Made with Théo Geiller , Anaïs Zafra and Lisa Martel , L'École de Design / 2019

Redesign of a website of e-learning of hacking.
How to valorise the penibility of the learning ?
During a week of workshop , we redesigned the concept of the website root-me.org to create an approach of the learning of hacking fully-gamified. The user won't only have to take up a challenge, but will challenge others players / apprentice - hacker of same level.
Based on game environments of MMO, the "player" will be encourage to improve his / her skills in hacking at every moment of his day : to win against others, he / she have to know perfectly the knowkings of his level !
Here we based the experience of learning on the parallel between progress in game, and improving of hacking skills ; but especially on the access of the documentation, very important in this environment : no given answers to newbies, because only the self investment in looking for answers, and the penibility of this tasks makes it as much gratifying.
So the user interface is centered on the progress of the player with access to his statistics from a lots of way, and the rarefaction of the documentation to give it a precius status, in order that the reading of those documentations became the goal of the "student".

Context

During a workshop of 4 days, we had to work in team of 5 students to redesign an existing website. Nammed "quick & dirty", the idea is to ends the week with a functional prototype, tested and verified. Centered on the UX part of the job, we had to identify the issues of root-me.org, a website of e-learning for hackers.

Personas

We divided in three types of users that Root-me could welcome on its platform : the "newbie" wich never coded before and have not any knowing about web security and web languages. One user warned concerning web languages but not really about hacking. Lastly, the warned user in hacking who want to train himself / herself or become more expert in some field.It permits to us to focus ours reflexions on those type of users, and develop a user journey especially for them.

Audit

Root-me is a portal of hacking exercices for self-learning, which requires motivation lever to pursue the education alone. But its interface is difficult at first, with a side bar and a top bar to navigate between menus, and a lot of submenus inside them. Whereas the documentation is central in this type of learning, it is not the first item that user can found on a exercice page. Gamified aspect is not fully exploited, with only few statistics at the end of the exercice. Finally, the community is only present of the chatbox on the side, whereas could be a motivation lever for hackers students. So from this audit we extracted a journey map for a new user on the platform.

This journey map was usefull to point the positive sides of the service, and localize the weaknesses that we have to fix.

Ideation

Because Root-me is a self-learning portal, we focused on the newer users that the platform could welcome. Firstly, we imagined a user journey based on daily MOOCs, but this idea was too floppy, and throwed away few times after to rechallenge it. because we are working on a computational subject, we thought that an approach fully-gamified could be an interesting way to encourage to improve his / her skills in hacking . The user won't only have to take up a challenge, but will challenge others players / apprentice - hacker of same level.
We develop this idea of the time consumed by it at every moment of the day : to win against others, he / she have to know perfectly the knowkings of his level !
The experience will be centered learning on the parallel between progress in game and improving of hacking skills by using a lots statistics . The documentation should not only a way to win a challenge, but also a goal for the player. He / she have to deserve it ! No given answers to newbies, because only the self investment in looking for answers, and the penibility of this task makes it as much gratifying.

Research & UX

We based the game environment on those ones of MMO, with an increased importance on documentation and statistics. To materialize his / her progress at every connection on the platform, home will present those elements and some loyalty levers as daily boxes. The in-game interface, will be represented as an arena where players could challenge themselves. At every step of its navigation, he / she should have a direct access to its documentation, and a visual on his / her progression.

Diagrams of the main interface wich gather player skills and progress in his learning - Image provided by Liv Danthon Lefebvre.

To proceed efficiently and quickly in the short time we had left, we work by periods of alone work, and group consultations to vote for the best idea concerning a functionality or the future axe we had to work on.

User tests

After finalized a prototype on Sketch, we tested it with some users in order to observe if they easily navigate in our service, if they understand well our entitles... We required that they verbalize their feelings during the test. It revealed that the access to documentation was not enough obvious on some interface, and users searched it. We proceeded with many iterations between prototyping and user tests to improve it until it seems fluent to each new user.

User tests with other students - Image provided by Liv Danthon Lefebvre.

Word of the end

The design sprint method was a good experimentation of team working during this workshop. It permits to bring out a new concept, design a viable prototype and test it in one week, in order to resolve an existing issue : the motivation drops during self-learning. Even if the final result need tests on targeted audience and a work on UI more developed than the basis we have done to test our prototype, it is quite satisfaying : 4 days centered on a idea, and we are able to determine its viability !
