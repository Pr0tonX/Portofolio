tabmaker

UX / UI

L'École de Design / 2018

Tabmaker get together musicians that need help or motivation to write, find and pursue tablatures.

It is a social platform where musicians could work together to create new tablatures of any song. Whereas writing tablatures could be usually tedious, users can be able to work on the same partition at the same time, proposing many ways, difficulty or version of a same song. Tabmaker aims to create a dynamic around the community of musicians who need help to write and pursue a tablature, but also for whose are more prolific and professional and want to share their works. In this way, "reader" people can be able to choose to read the tablature following the classic way or choosing every part of the transcription that they prefer, or need according to their level.

Context

Musicians - and moreover self-taught people - are always looking for new tablatures, seeking for the ONE that is the most respectful of the original sound, one wich match with their guitar level or one wich offer an unconventional version. But this search could be laborious when they search a particular one, from a song not famous, or the live version of an awesome solo. Trying to write down his own tablature is often long and could be demoralizing for novices, who can not find a proper help to their issues.

Ideation

Tabmaker will be a service wich provide a solution for many guitar players and could be a new platform of self-promotion for profesionnals musicians : a sharing platform wich integrates an collaborative tool to write new tablatures or edit old ones.
The editor will be a collaborative tool, where many users could work on it at the same time. Due to its collaborative aspect, the tablature editor should have a functionnality that permits users to write many versions of a same part of the partition ; in order to let the choice to readers between them.
The chat, always present in the navigation, allows editors to talks about their work while they are writing, readers to ask some tips / help, and even more.

Research & UX

I started by a market research wich revealed that some actors had developped desktop apps for tablature editing, and many web portals of pre existing partitions already exist. So, it is time to blend them and create a collaborative tool !
I started by listing every necessary functionnality for a collaborative tool for partitions, and then add some usefull features, as a weekly vote to choose the next tab which will be edited on the coming community rendez-vous. I fixed the main navigation on the website, and the hierarchization of functionnalities units to reduce the cognitive burden of this web app.

I made a first prototype with Adobe XD, and after some tests, I saw that the navigation was pertubed by a density of information too important. Many iterations to fix it and create a smooth navigation.

UI design

I used dark colors because I care about my user's eyes, and the use of Tabmaker should be long, so I chose a constrast dark brown - beige to keep this feeling of authenticity that we have when touching instruments. Some informations is added to the top of the partition, both for editor and reader, to have a short resume of it and put it in context. To choose between to version of a phrase, reader will have to drag and drop on the choosen version, wich could be lisenend before.

Word of the end

Tabmaker is a project wich brings a new service in order to answer to a real issue for musicians , and try to offer a service wich could be used as by experimented users ( self-promotion, for example) as novices. It have the ambition to create a community around it, and galvanise the learning of music with a social stimulant. The only regret I have about this project was the lack of time because interfaces remain untested by a larger sample group of musicians, yet I am curious to see how real users will interact on this new platform.
